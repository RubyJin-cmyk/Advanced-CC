Fragmented Self Camera

Project Description

Fragmented Self Camera turns portrait making into a process of exaggeration and recombination. By colliding feminine and masculine elements, it produces unique results that reveal the layered nature of identity.

Design Process

The initial UI lacked guidance and did not clearly communicate what kind of camera it was. I refined the interface by clarifying prompts, adding more assets, and improving face detection from loose capture to eye-based framing.

Reflection

Images are shaped by intention, not objectivity. This camera pushes against repetitive, overly perfect selfies by introducing distortion and playfulness, while using state-based logic to keep interaction clear across stages.

Interactive Typography

Series Description

This series explores interactive typography through motion, scale, and user input. The works investigate how text can shift from static reading into a responsive and expressive visual form.

Process

The works developed through iteration—testing different structures, adjusting movement, and refining interaction. Each piece balances control and unpredictability, focusing on rhythm, composition, and user engagement.

Reflection

Through this series, I explored how small changes in motion and interaction can transform the perception of text. I became more interested in typography as something spatial and performative, rather than purely readable.
